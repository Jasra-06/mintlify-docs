## What We Evaluate

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="🧠 Learning & Curiosity" icon="brain">
    Do you engage deeply with prerequisites, TeachGrid, and EdTech concepts? Are you growing every day?
  </Card>

  <Card title="💬 Communication" icon="comments">
    Are you showing up, submitting EOD updates, and communicating clearly — without being chased?
  </Card>

  <Card title="🚀 Initiative" icon="rocket">
    Do you go beyond the minimum? Do you bring ideas, spot issues, and take ownership?
  </Card>

  <Card title="🤝 Collaboration" icon="people-group">
    Are you a positive team presence? Do you help fellow interns and engage constructively?
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

## What Great Looks Like 🌟

| Behavior | Good Intern | Great Intern |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Attendance | Shows up most days | Never misses, joins early |
| EOD Update | Submits when reminded | Submits daily without fail |
| Blockers | Waits to mention | Flags same day in the meet |
| Feedback | Receives it passively | Acts on it fast and visibly |
| TeachGrid | Visits once | Explores deeply, asks questions |
| Presentation | Covers the topics | Shares genuine insights and ideas |

## Feedback Timeline

<Steps>
  <Step title="Daily Meets">
    Informal check-ins every morning. Your mentor observes consistency, attitude, and engagement.
  </Step>
  <Step title="First Week Presentation">
    Your first milestone evaluation. Quality, curiosity, and confidence are observed.
  </Step>
  <Step title="End of Program">
    Mentor shares written feedback on your overall performance, growth, and contribution.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## The Mindset That Wins 💡

> _"You don't need to be the most experienced person in the room. You need to be the most consistent, curious, and coachable one."_

- 🔁 Show up daily — consistency is visible
- 🙋 Ask early — waiting costs everyone time
- ✅ Act on feedback — it shows growth
- 💡 Bring ideas — even "junior" ones matter
- 🧠 Stay curious — every task is a learning opportunity

<Tip>
  The interns who stand out the most aren't always the most skilled — they're the most reliable and eager to learn.
</Tip>
