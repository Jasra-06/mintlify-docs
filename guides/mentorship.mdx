## What Your Mentor Does

Your assigned mentor is your primary point of contact for:

- Daily task guidance and feedback
- Answering questions about TeachGrid, EdTech, and your work
- Conducting the 10 AM daily meet
- Reviewing your EOD updates and presentation
- Supporting your overall growth during the program

---

## How to Work With Your Mentor

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Come Prepared 📋" icon="clipboard-list">
    Bring questions, blockers, and updates to every meet. Don't wait to be asked.
  </Card>

  <Card title="Be Specific 🎯" icon="target">
    "I'm stuck on X, tried Y and Z, getting this error" is far better than "I need help."
  </Card>

  <Card title="Act on Feedback ✅" icon="check-circle">
    When your mentor gives feedback, apply it fast and close the loop with them.
  </Card>

  <Card title="Ask Early 🙋" icon="hand">
    Stuck for more than 1–2 hours? Ask. Don't burn half a day on something your mentor can unblock in 5 minutes.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

---

## How to Ask Great Questions

The best questions give your mentor everything they need to help you fast:

```text
I'm trying to: [what you want to accomplish]
I've already tried: [what you attempted]
What I'm seeing: [the result / error]
My best guess: [what you think might be causing it]
```

<Tip>
  Writing this out often helps you figure out the answer yourself — and it makes your mentor's job much easier.
</Tip>

---

## Daily Meet Etiquette 🕙

Every morning at **10:00 AM on Teams**:

- Join by **9:58 AM** — camera on, mic ready
- Share: what you did yesterday, what you're doing today, any blockers
- Listen actively — your teammates' updates are relevant to you too
- DM your mentor **before 10 AM** if you can't make it

## Building a Real Relationship 🌱

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Show genuine curiosity" icon="brain">
    Ask your mentor what they're working on and what excites them about HashInkludes. People love talking about their work when someone asks thoughtfully.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Share your perspective" icon="comments">
    Share your honest observations about TeachGrid, EdTech, or your tasks — even if you're uncertain. Fresh perspectives are valuable.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Thank them specifically" icon="heart">
    "Thanks for your help!" is forgettable. "Your explanation of ICPs helped me structure my whole presentation" is memorable.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

<Note>
  If you have any concerns about your mentor relationship or program experience, reach out to the program coordinator confidentially. Raising concerns early leads to better outcomes.
</Note>
